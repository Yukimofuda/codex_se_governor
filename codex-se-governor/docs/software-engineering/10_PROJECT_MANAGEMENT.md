# Project Management

## Purpose

Keep software work visible, estimable, monitored, and coordinated.

## Concepts from PDF

- Project planning covers tasks, milestones, deliverables, schedule, buffers, resources, and communication.
- Activity networks, critical path, Gantt charts, monitoring, and reporting expose dependencies.
- People management and stakeholder engagement affect delivery quality.
- Scrum and agile project management use backlog, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives.
- AI can assist planning but cannot own accountability.

## Codex Rules

- Break non-trivial work into tasks and deliverables.
- Record risks that affect schedule, scope, quality, or people.
- Keep stakeholder-visible artifacts current.
- Use retrospectives to propose rule or memory updates.

## Required Outputs

- Task scaffold for significant work.
- Risk register.
- Final report with remaining work and risks.

## Checklist

- [ ] Milestone or delivery boundary is clear.
- [ ] Dependencies are visible.
- [ ] Estimate uncertainty is stated.
- [ ] Stakeholder impact is described.
- [ ] Retrospective captures learning.

## Anti-patterns

- Treating a large task as one invisible coding step.
- Ignoring schedule risk from testing, review, or integration.
- Adding people or AI automation without coordination cost.
